## Overview

Forests have been revered across cultures as dwelling places of spirits, sites of mystery, sources of wisdom, and natural temples. From druids' oak groves to Shinto shrine forests, from Buddha's bodhi tree to the Amazon's green cathedral, trees and forests connect earth to sky, roots to crown, human to divine.

## Types of Sacred Forests

### Temple Forests
Forests surrounding and protecting temples, monasteries, shrines

### Sacred Groves
Small woodlands set aside as holy, protected by taboo

### Ancient Tree Sites
Individual trees of great age and spiritual significance

### Vision Quest Forests
Wilderness areas used for spiritual retreat and seeking

### Shamanic Power Spots
Forest locations known for heightened spiritual energy

## Forest Conservation as Sacred Practice

### Traditional Protection
- Sacred groves protected by taboo, community law
- Punishment for harm: spiritual and social consequences
- Biodiversity protection through spirituality

### Contemporary Sacred Ecology
- Forest monks ordaining trees (Thailand)
- Indigenous land rights movements
- Spiritual environmentalism
- Treesitting as prayer

### Threats to Sacred Forests
- Logging, development
- Climate change
- Loss of traditional knowledge
- Breaking of taboos

### How Pilgrims Can Help
- Support indigenous forest protection
- Choose eco-tourism operators
- Don't remove anything from sacred forests
- Spread awareness of sacred groves
- Donate to forest conservation
- Respect access restrictions

## Forest Etiquette

### Do:
- Walk quietly
- Stay on paths
- Observe without disturbing
- Take time to be present
- Ask permission before entering sacred groves
- Follow local customs for offerings
- Leave no trace

### Don't:
- Break branches
- Carve into trees
- Remove plants, stones, artifacts
- Disturb wildlife
- Play loud music
- Leave trash
- Enter restricted sacred groves
