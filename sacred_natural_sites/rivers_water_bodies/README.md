## Overview

Water is universally sacred: purifying, life-sustaining, transformative. Sacred rivers carry prayers to the ocean, holy lakes reflect the sky, springs burst forth with healing power, waterfalls thunder with cosmic energy. Pilgrims bathe in these waters, drink them, meditate beside them, and follow them on sacred journeys.

## Sacred Rivers

### Asia

#### Ganges (India)
- **Sanskrit**: Ganga
- **Origin**: Gangotri Glacier (Himalayas)
- **Length**: 2,525 km
- **Significance**: Holiest river in Hinduism, descended from heaven
- **Sacred Cities**: Haridwar, Varanasi, Prayagraj, Rishikesh
- **Rituals**: Bathing for purification, cremation ghats, Ganga Aarti ceremonies
- **Pilgrimage**: Following river from source to delta

#### Yamuna (India)
- **Origin**: Yamunotri Glacier
- **Significance**: Associated with Krishna, sister of Yama (death)
- **Meeting**: Sangam at Prayagraj (confluence with Ganges)
- **Cities**: Mathura, Vrindavan, Agra
- **Current Challenge**: Severe pollution requires spiritual and practical healing

#### Narmada (India)
- **Flow**: East to west (unusual)
- **Significance**: Born from Shiva's body
- **Pilgrimage**: 2,600km parikrama (circumambulation) on both banks
- **Cities**: Omkareshwar, Maheshwar, sacred marble rocks

#### Brahmaputra (Tibet/India/Bangladesh)
- **Name**: "Son of Brahma"
- **Journey**: Tibet through Himalayan gorges to India
- **Significance**: Life-giver to millions, island river culture
- **Power**: Dramatic seasonal changes, flooding, renewal

#### Yangtze (China)
- **Length**: 6,300 km (longest in Asia)
- **Significance**: Mother river of Chinese civilization
- **Sacred Sites**: Three Gorges, river temples
- **Goddess**: River deity worship

#### Yellow River/Huang He (China)
- **Significance**: Cradle of Chinese civilization
- **Challenge**: "China's Sorrow" due to flooding
- **Ancient Sites**: Along river valley

#### Mekong (Southeast Asia)
- **Countries**: 6 nations (China to Vietnam)
- **Significance**: Buddhist cultures, Naga serpent myths
- **Life**: Biodiversity hotspot, sustains millions
- **Power Spots**: Waterfalls, rapids, source region
