## Overview

Caves have served as humanity's first shelters and temples. Dark, mysterious, and womb-like, they represent descent into the unconscious, journey to the underworld, death and rebirth, and the earth's generative power. From prehistoric painted galleries to Buddhist meditation caves to Christian hermitages, caves are threshold spaces between worlds.

## Cave Safety & Etiquette

### Safety
- **Never Alone**: Cave with experienced guide
- **Equipment**: Proper lights, backup lights, helmet
- **Stay on Path**: Easy to get lost
- **Claustrophobia**: Know your limits
- **Flooding**: Danger in rainy seasons
- **Air Quality**: Some caves have poor ventilation

### Respect
- **Don't Touch**: Oils from hands damage formations
- **No Trash**: Pack everything out
- **Quiet**: Respect sacred atmosphere
- **Photography**: Check rules, no flash on ancient art
- **Offerings**: Only if permitted and biodegradable
- **Restricted Areas**: Honor closed sections

### Conservation
- **White-Nose Syndrome**: Bats threatened, decontamination protocols
- **Fragile Formations**: Thousands of years to form, seconds to destroy
- **Microorganisms**: Cave ecosystems easily disrupted
- **Ancient Art**: Irreplaceable, damaged by breath moisture, flash, touch
