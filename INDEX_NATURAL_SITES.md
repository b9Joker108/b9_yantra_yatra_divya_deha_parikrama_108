## Introduction

Beyond human-made temples and monuments, nature itself offers sacred spaces: mountains that pierce the sky, rivers that cleanse and sustain, forests that shelter and teach, caves that hold ancient mysteries, deserts that strip away pretense, and islands that serve as isolated sanctuaries. This index organizes Earth's natural sacred sites by feature type.

## Practices for Natural Sites

### Universal Natural Site Practices
- **Presence** - Full attention to place
- **Offerings** - Respectful, biodegradable
- **Circumambulation** - Walking around sacred features
- **Meditation** - Sitting with natural energy
- **Prayer** - Communication with divine in nature
- **Vision Quest** - Solo wilderness time
- **Pilgrimage** - Journey to natural sacred sites
- **Stewardship** - Care for sacred places

### Leave No Trace Principles
1. Plan ahead and prepare
2. Travel and camp on durable surfaces
3. Dispose of waste properly
4. Leave what you find
5. Minimize campfire impacts
6. Respect wildlife
7. Be considerate of other visitors
