## Sanskrit/Hindu Terms

**Apu** - Mountain spirit or deity in Andean tradition (also spelled 'apu')  
**Ashram** - Spiritual hermitage or monastery; place of spiritual practice  
**Atman** - The eternal self, individual soul, divine essence within  
**Bhakti** - Devotion, devotional love for the divine  
**Brahman** - Ultimate reality, cosmic consciousness, the Absolute  
**Chakra** - Energy centers in the subtle body (7 major chakras)  
**Dana** - Generosity, giving, especially to monastics  
**Darshan** - Sacred viewing, seeing and being seen by the divine  
**Dharma** - Cosmic law, duty, righteousness, the path  
**Ghat** - Steps leading to a river, especially for bathing/cremation  
**Guru** - Spiritual teacher (literally: "one who brings light from darkness")  
**Japa** - Repetition of mantras or divine names  
**Karma** - Action and its consequences, cause and effect  
**Kirtan** - Devotional singing, chanting of divine names  
**Linga** - Abstract representation of Shiva, often stone pillar  
**Mala** - Prayer beads (typically 108 beads)  
**Mandala** - Sacred geometric pattern representing the cosmos  
**Mandir** - Hindu temple  
**Mantra** - Sacred sound or phrase repeated in meditation  
**Maya** - Illusion, the veil that obscures ultimate reality  
**Moksha** - Liberation from the cycle of rebirth  
**Mudra** - Sacred hand gesture with symbolic meaning  
**Murti** - Sacred image or statue of a deity  
**Namaste** - "I bow to the divine in you"  
**Om/Aum** - Sacred primordial sound, symbol of ultimate reality  
**Parikrama/Pradakshina** - Circumambulation, walking around sacred sites clockwise  
**Prasad** - Blessed food offered to deity then shared with devotees  
**Puja** - Worship ritual, offering ceremony  
**Purana** - Ancient Hindu texts containing mythology and legends  
**Sadhu** - Holy person, renunciant, ascetic  
**Samadhi** - Deep meditative absorption; also tomb of a saint  
**Samsara** - The cycle of birth, death, and rebirth  
**Sangam** - Confluence of rivers  
**Sannyasi** - Renunciant who has taken vows  
**Seva** - Selfless service  
**Shakti** - Divine feminine power, energy  
**Shanti** - Peace  
**Shiva** - The destroyer/transformer deity; Supreme Being  
**Tirth/Tirtha** - Sacred crossing place, pilgrimage site  
**Upanishads** - Philosophical texts, part of the Vedas  
**Veda** - Ancient sacred texts of Hinduism  
**Yantra** - Sacred geometric diagram used in meditation  
**Yoga** - Union; spiritual practices for connecting with the divine  
**Yogi/Yogini** - Practitioner of yoga (male/female)

## Buddhist Terms

**Bodhi** - Awakening, enlightenment  
**Bodhisattva** - One who seeks enlightenment for the benefit of all beings  
**Buddha** - "Awakened one"; the historical Buddha (Siddhartha Gautama) or enlightened being  
**Dharma** - The teachings of the Buddha; cosmic law  
**Dukkha** - Suffering, unsatisfactoriness  
**Gompa** - Tibetan Buddhist monastery  
**Koan** - Paradoxical question used in Zen practice  
**Kora** - Tibetan circumambulation of sacred sites  
**Lama** - Tibetan Buddhist teacher  
**Mandala** - Sacred circular diagram representing the cosmos  
**Mantra** - Sacred phrase (e.g., "Om Mani Padme Hum")  
**Metta** - Loving-kindness  
**Nirvana** - Liberation from suffering, enlightenment  
**Pagoda** - Tiered tower, often containing relics  
**Rinpoche** - "Precious one," honorific for high lamas  
**Sangha** - Buddhist community  
**Stupa** - Dome-shaped monument containing relics  
**Sutra** - Buddhist scripture  
**Thangka** - Tibetan Buddhist painted scroll  
**Vihara** - Buddhist monastery  
**Wat** - Buddhist temple (Thailand, Laos, Cambodia)  
**Zazen** - Seated Zen meditation  
**Zen** - School of Buddhism emphasizing meditation (Chan in Chinese)

## Islamic Terms

**Allah** - God (Arabic)  
**Dervish** - Sufi mystic  
**Dhikr/Zikr** - Remembrance of God through repetition  
**Hajj** - Pilgrimage to Mecca (5th pillar of Islam)  
**Imam** - Prayer leader; spiritual leader  
**Kaaba** - Cubic building in Mecca, holiest site in Islam  
**Madrasa** - Islamic school  
**Masjid** - Mosque  
**Mihrab** - Prayer niche indicating direction of Mecca  
**Minaret** - Tower from which call to prayer is made  
**Qibla** - Direction of Mecca for prayer  
**Quran/Koran** - Holy book of Islam  
**Ramadan** - Month of fasting  
**Salat** - Ritual prayer (5 times daily)  
**Sufi** - Islamic mystic  
**Umrah** - Lesser pilgrimage to Mecca  
**Wudu** - Ritual ablution before prayer  
**Zawiya** - Sufi lodge or monastery

## Christian Terms

**Abbey** - Monastery headed by an abbot/abbess  
**Basilica** - Important church with special status  
**Cathedral** - Principal church of a diocese, bishop's seat  
**Chapel** - Small place of worship  
**Hermitage** - Dwelling of a hermit; secluded monastery  
**Icon** - Sacred image in Eastern Christianity  
**Monastery** - Community of monks  
**Pilgrim** - One on religious journey  
**Relic** - Physical remains or objects of saints  
**Shrine** - Sacred place often containing relics  
**Stations of the Cross** - Series depicting Jesus's crucifixion journey

## Jewish Terms

**Bar/Bat Mitzvah** - Coming of age ceremony  
**Kippah/Yarmulke** - Head covering worn by men  
**Menorah** - Seven-branched candelabrum  
**Mezuzah** - Doorpost scroll with Torah verses  
**Rabbi** - Jewish teacher and spiritual leader  
**Shabbat** - Sabbath (Friday evening to Saturday evening)  
**Synagogue** - House of worship  
**Tallit** - Prayer shawl  
**Tefillin** - Phylacteries worn during prayer  
**Torah** - Five Books of Moses; Jewish law  
**Western Wall/Kotel** - Remains of Second Temple in Jerusalem  
**Yeshiva** - Jewish religious school

## Natural Features

**Cairn** - Stone pile marking sacred or significant spot

**Grove** - Small wood of trees, often sacred  
**Mountain** - Elevated land, universally considered sacred  
**Spring** - Water source emerging from earth, often holy  
**Waterfall** - Cascade of water, place of power
