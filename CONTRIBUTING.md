## Welcome

Thank you for your interest in contributing to the Global Parikrama project! While this is primarily a personal pilgrimage repository, thoughtful contributions that enhance the documentation, correct errors, or add valuable insights are warmly welcomed.

## Philosophy

This repository approaches sacred sites with:
- **Reverence** - Deep respect for all traditions
- **Accuracy** - Well-researched, factual information
- **Beginner's Mind** - Openness and humility
- **Beauty** - Aesthetic presentation that honors the sacred
- **Inclusivity** - Welcoming all genuine spiritual seekers

## Types of Contributions

### ‚úì Welcome Contributions

**Corrections & Updates:**
- Factual errors in site descriptions
- Outdated visiting information (hours, fees, access)
- Broken links
- Spelling and grammar improvements

**Enhancements:**
- Additional historical context
- Cultural insights from practitioners
- Practical tips for fellow pilgrims
- Safety warnings or access changes
- Updated regulations or permit requirements

**Resources:**
- Relevant books, documentaries, websites
- Recommended guides (with permission)
- Maps and route information
- Translation help

**Respectful Photography:**
- High-quality images (with proper permissions)
- Photos that follow the photography guidelines
- Credited and captioned appropriately

### ‚úó Not Appropriate

**Please Don't:**
- Add promotional content (tour companies, hotels, products)
- Include disrespectful or mocking content
- Share sacred information meant to remain private
- Add sites without proper research
- Copy-paste from other sources without permission
- Include your personal social media for self-promotion
- Engage in religious debates or proselytizing
- Add sites you haven't personally visited (unless clearly marked as research)

## How to Contribute

### Small Changes
For minor corrections (typos, broken links, small factual updates):
1. Fork the repository
2. Make your changes
3. Submit a pull request with clear description
4. Wait for review

### Larger Contributions
For significant additions (new site documentation, major content):
1. Open an issue first describing what you'd like to add
2. Discuss the contribution with repository maintainer
3. Once approved, fork and create your content
4. Follow the repository style and templates
5. Submit pull request with detailed description

### Documentation Standards

**When documenting sites:**
- Use the provided [Site Documentation Template](resources/site_documentation_template.md)
- Include sources for historical/factual claims
- Distinguish between fact, tradition, and personal experience
- Use respectful language
- Follow the [Sacred Site Etiquette](practices_protocols/sacred_site_etiquette.md) guidelines

**Writing Style:**
- Clear, accessible language
- Culturally sensitive terminology
- Avoid jargon (or define it in the glossary)
- Respectful tone throughout
- First-person for personal experiences, third-person for factual content

### Photography Contributions

**Requirements:**
- You own the photo or have explicit permission to share it
- Photo follows [Photography Guidelines](practices_protocols/photography_guidelines.md)
- No photography from prohibited areas
- No photos of people without consent
- High resolution and well-composed
- Include: Location, date, any relevant context
- Specify license (preferably Creative Commons Attribution)

## Cultural Sensitivity

### Essential Guidelines

**Do:**
- Research before writing
- Consult primary sources from the tradition itself
- Acknowledge when you're an outsider
- Use appropriate terminology from the tradition
- Respect sacred secrets (don't share everything)
- Credit indigenous and local knowledge
- Recognize power dynamics and privilege

**Don't:**
- Appropriate sacred practices or symbols
- Claim expertise you don't have
- Share information that community members have asked to keep private
- Use outdated or offensive terminology
- Reduce complex traditions to stereotypes
- Ignore indigenous perspectives

### Religious Neutrality

This repository respects all genuine spiritual traditions:
- No proselytizing for any religion
- No dismissing others' beliefs
- No "my tradition is best" attitudes
- Present traditions on their own terms
- Acknowledge diversity within traditions

## Fact-Checking

**For factual claims, include:**
- Sources (books, websites, personal interviews)
- Date of information (especially for practical details)
- Your relationship to the information (visited personally, researched, told by guide, etc.)

**Be clear about:**
- History vs. mythology
- Tradition vs. historical fact
- Personal interpretation vs. established teaching

## Respecting Indigenous Knowledge

**Special considerations for indigenous sites:**
- Prioritize indigenous voices and sources
- Don't share sacred locations that tribes wish to keep private
- Acknowledge land and people
- Support indigenous-led tourism when suggesting visits
- Recognize colonialism's impacts
- Include indigenous names alongside colonial names

## Language & Translation

**Contributions in languages other than English:**
- Very welcome! Especially for sites in your home country
- Please include English translation or summary
- Help with translating existing content appreciated

**Terminology:**
- Use terms from the tradition itself (Sanskrit for Hindu sites, Arabic for Islamic, etc.)
- Include definitions or link to glossary
- Spell names according to standard transliteration

## Review Process

**What to expect:**
1. Your contribution will be reviewed for:
   - Accuracy
   - Cultural sensitivity
   - Alignment with repository philosophy
   - Quality of writing/images
   - Appropriate sourcing

2. Feedback may be provided for revisions

3. Once approved, your contribution will be merged

4. You'll be credited as a contributor

## Code of Conduct

**Be:**
- Respectful
- Humble
- Kind
- Open to feedback
- Patient with review process

**Don't be:**
- Disrespectful of any tradition
- Pushy or demanding
- Dismissive of feedback
- Defensive about corrections

## Attribution

**Contributors will be acknowledged:**
- In commit history
- In a CONTRIBUTORS file (if they wish)
- In photo credits
- In site documentation where appropriate

**What we need:**
- How you'd like to be credited
- Website/social media (optional)
- Your relationship to the contribution (visited, researched, etc.)

## Licensing

By contributing, you agree that your contributions will be licensed under the same GPL v3.0 license as the rest of the repository. Photos should ideally be under Creative Commons Attribution license.

## Questions?

If you're unsure about whether your contribution fits, open an issue to discuss before doing the work. Better to ask first than have contributions declined later.

## Special Requests

**Help especially needed with:**
- Translation of content
- Local insights on sites in your region
- Updates to changed access/hours/regulations
- Proofreading for cultural sensitivity
- Indigenous perspectives

## Gratitude

Every thoughtful contribution honors the sacred sites we document. Thank you for helping create a beautiful, respectful, and useful resource for pilgrims worldwide.
