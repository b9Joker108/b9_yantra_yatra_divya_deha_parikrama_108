## Overview

Africa's sacred landscape spans from the pyramids of Egypt to the rock churches of Ethiopia, from Sufi zawiyas to animist power spots. The continent holds humanity's oldest spiritual expressions, ancient mystery traditions, and vibrant living practices that honor the interconnection of all life.

## Sacred Site Types

### Ancient Temples & Monuments
Egyptian temples, rock-hewn churches, Great Zimbabwe, megalithic structures

### Islamic Sacred Sites
Grand mosques, Sufi zawiya complexes, marabout tombs, medina sanctuaries

### Indigenous Sacred Spaces
Animist groves, ancestral shrines, rock art sites, sacred natural features

### Natural Power Spots
Mountains, caves, rivers, lakes, deserts, ancient forests

## Spiritual Traditions

### Ancient Egyptian Mysteries
Temple complexes preserving hermetic wisdom, initiation chambers, sacred geometry

### Sufism
Islamic mysticism with zawiya centers, tomb shrines, dhikr gathering places

### Traditional African Religions
Yoruba, Vodun, Akan, San, Maasai, and hundreds of indigenous traditions

### Ethiopian Christianity
Ancient Orthodox churches, monasteries, rock-hewn sanctuaries

### Ancestral Veneration
Connections to lineage, sacred burial sites, communication with ancestors

## Special Considerations

### Political Stability
Some regions have access challenges due to conflicts. Research current conditions carefully.

### Climate & Seasons
- North Africa: October-April for desert regions
- East Africa: Year-round, avoiding heavy rains
- West Africa: November-February (dry season)
- Southern Africa: May-September (winter)

### Cultural Protocols
Respect for elders, permission for sacred site access, appropriate dress, photography permissions
