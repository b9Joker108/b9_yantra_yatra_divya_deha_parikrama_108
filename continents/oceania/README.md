## Indigenous Traditions

### Aboriginal Australian
Hundreds of nations with distinct dreaming stories, songlines, and law (lore). World's oldest continuous culture.

### MƒÅori
Polynesian culture of Aotearoa with deep connection to land (whenua), ancestors (t≈´puna), and spiritual power (mana).

### Pacific Islander
Diverse Melanesian, Polynesian, and Micronesian traditions with navigation, tattooing, kava ceremonies, and ancestral veneration.

## Special Considerations

### Cultural Sensitivity
- Many sites are sacred and restricted to indigenous people
- Photography often prohibited
- Climbing Uluru now banned (returned to traditional owners)
- Always seek permission and hire indigenous guides where available

### Remote Access
Many sites require 4WD, flights, or boats. Some accessible only in dry season.

### Climate & Seasons
- Australian Outback: May-September (avoid extreme summer heat)
- Tropical North: May-October (dry season)
- New Zealand: October-April (spring-autumn)
- Pacific Islands: Year-round, but cyclone season November-April

### Quarantine & Biosecurity
Australia and New Zealand have strict quarantine rules. Declare all organic materials.
