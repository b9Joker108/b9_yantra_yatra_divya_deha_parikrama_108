## Access & Practical Information

### How to Visit
- **Expedition Cruises** - Most common, from Ushuaia (Argentina) or Punta Arenas (Chile)
- **Fly-Cruise Options** - Skip the Drake Passage
- **Research Station Visits** - Limited opportunities
- **Adventure Tourism** - Kayaking, camping, mountaineering (expert level)

### When to Visit
- **November-December** - Spring, courtship season, longer days
- **January-February** - Peak summer, penguin chicks, best weather
- **March** - Autumn light, whale watching peaks, beginning of ice formation

### Treaty Considerations
The Antarctic Treaty (1961) designates Antarctica as a scientific preserve, establishes freedom of scientific investigation, and bans military activity. Tourism is permitted but strictly regulated.

### Leave No Trace
IAATO (International Association of Antarctica Tour Operators) guidelines:
- No touching wildlife
- Maintain distance (5m from penguins, 15m from seals)
- Take nothing, leave nothing
- No introduction of non-native species
- Stay on designated paths

### Cost
Antarctic expeditions are expensive ($5,000-$50,000+), reflecting the logistical challenges and environmental protections.
